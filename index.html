Understanding Sleep: The Science Behind Better Rest

Sleep isn't just about hours — it's about cycles, stages, and timing. Here's what you need to know.

Why Sleep Matters

Sleep is essential for physical recovery, cognitive function, emotional regulation, and immune health. It's not optional — it's biological necessity.

Sleep Cycles

Each night, your brain moves through repeating 90-minute sleep cycles. Waking at the end of a cycle — not in the middle — makes all the difference.

REM Sleep

REM (rapid eye movement) sleep is when most dreaming occurs. It supports memory consolidation, emotional processing, and learning.

Deep Sleep

Deep sleep is the physically restorative stage. It's when your body repairs tissue, releases growth hormone, and strengthens your immune system.

Circadian Rhythm

Your internal 24-hour clock regulates sleep-wake cycles. Consistent wake times and morning light exposure help keep it aligned.

Healthy Sleep Habits

Dim lights, cool rooms, consistent schedules, and screen-free wind-down routines are the foundation of good sleep hygiene.

Sleep Duration Recommendations

Most adults need 7-9 hours. Teenagers need 8-10. Younger children and older adults have different needs — age matters.
